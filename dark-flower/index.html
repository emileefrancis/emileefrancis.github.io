Washington Native Plant

Pacific Rhododendron

Rhododendron macrophyllum

Pacific rhododendron is a flowering evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest. It is known for its large pink blossoms, broad leathery leaves, and ability to grow well in cool, moist forest environments.

Alternate Names

Coast rhododendron, big-leaf rhododendron, California rhododendron, and California rosebay.

Physical Size

Usually grows 3–15 feet tall, but can reach up to 25–30 feet in ideal conditions.

Geographic Range

Found from southern British Columbia through Washington and Oregon into California.

Basic Info

Climate

It grows best in mild, moist climates like the Pacific Northwest, especially in partial shade and well-drained acidic soil.

Habitat

Pacific rhododendron is often found in forest understories, woodland edges, and open mountain slopes.

Quick Description

  1. It is an evergreen shrub with broad, leathery green leaves.
  2. Its flowers are usually pink to rose-purple and bloom in late spring.
  3. It is the state flower of Washington.
  4. It thrives in cool, damp woodland environments.
