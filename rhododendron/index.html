Pacific Rhododendron

Rhododendron macrophyllum

Basic Info

Alternate names: Coast rhododendron, Big-leaf rhododendron, California rhododendron, California rosebay

Physical size: Usually about 3–15 feet tall, but can grow up to around 25–30 feet in ideal conditions.

Geographic range: Found from southern British Columbia through Washington and Oregon into California (especially west of the Cascades and along the coast).

Best climate: Mild and moist climates like the Pacific Northwest. It grows best with partial shade and well-drained, slightly acidic soil.

Quick Description

Pacific rhododendron is an evergreen shrub with large leathery leaves and clusters of pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It often grows in forest understories and woodland edges.
